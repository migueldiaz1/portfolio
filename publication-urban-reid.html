Urban Object Re-Identification

Our 1st Place winning solution (on the private leaderboard) for the ICIP 2025 Grand Challenge, based on data-centric and model-centric enhancements.

Project Summary

In this study, we proposed an enhanced urban object re-identification (ReID) pipeline based on the 'Bag-of-Tricks' (BoT) framework. We introduced a variety of contributions at different levels, including dataset-level enhancements (super-resolution, style transfer, and data augmentation) and architectural enhancements to the BoT baseline.

The Challenge

Urban object ReID (like containers or bins) is a particularly difficult problem. Unlike person ReID, it presents unique challenges:

  • Low inter-class variability: Many objects are visually identical due to shared manufacturers.
  • High intra-class variability: The same object can look drastically different due to weather, lighting, vandalism, or viewpoint changes.

Our Solution

Our strategy combined data-centric and model-centric enhancements to create a robust pipeline.

1. Data-Centric Enhancements

  • Super-Resolution (Real-ESRGAN): We used a GAN model to upscale low-resolution images to a high-quality, distortion-free input size.
  • Style Transfer (U-Net): We created a hybrid dataset combining the high-frequency details from super-resolved images with the natural textures of the original domain.
  • Data Augmentation: We applied perspective distortion, lighting adjustments, rotations, and patch occlusions to simulate real-world conditions.

2. Model-Centric Enhancements

  • Backbone Optimization: We evaluated several architectures, finding that SE-ResNet-50 significantly outperformed the baseline, achieving a +5.36 mAP improvement.
  • Reranking (QMV): We applied a Query Majority Voting strategy that grouped adjacent frames to improve classification consistency.
